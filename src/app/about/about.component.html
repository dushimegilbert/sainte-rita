Background of the School

Ecole Sainte Rita: A Legacy of Vision and Education

Ecole Sainte Rita is a school located in Gasabo district, Bumbogo Sector, a few meters ahead of AZAM. It has Nursery and Primary sections. It started with 25 students in 2018 and now has 350 students in both sections with a school motto: “Education est la fondation de la culture et de la science.”

Ecole Sainte Rita stands as a testament to the enduring power of dedicated educators and a commitment to holistic student development. Founded by a collective of ten passionate teachers known as “Teachers’ Vision,” the school's genesis lies in a shared belief in the transformative potential of education and a desire to create a nurturing and stimulating learning environment.

Origin of Teachers’ Vision

The foundation of Ecole Sainte Rita can be traced back to the collaborative efforts of these ten teachers, “Teachers’ Vision,” a group of educators united by a common ethos, and advised by Spiritual Father Didier (Regina Pacis Remera).

  • Academic Excellence: A commitment to providing a rigorous and challenging curriculum designed to cultivate critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and a thirst for knowledge.
  • Holistic Development: A recognition of the importance of nurturing the whole child – intellectually, emotionally, socially, and physically.
  • Nurturing Environment: A dedication to creating a safe, supportive, and inclusive atmosphere where students feel valued, respected, and encouraged to reach their full potential.
  • Community Engagement: A belief in the power of collaboration and a commitment to fostering strong relationships between the school, parents, and the wider community.
  • Shared Vision: The teachers envisioned a school where innovation flourished, where students were empowered to become responsible and engaged citizens, and where the pursuit of knowledge was a shared journey.

History of Sainte Rita of Cascia

St. Rita: Patron Saint of the Impossible

Sainte Rita of Cascia, born on May 22, 1381, in Roccaporena, Italy, was an Italian widow and Augustinian nun known for her deep faith and devotion. Forced into marriage with a violent husband, she influenced him to change, but he was later murdered. Choosing forgiveness, she encouraged her sons to do the same. After their deaths, she joined the community in Cascia, dedicating her life to prayer and service. She received a mystical wound symbolizing Christ’s suffering. She died in 1457 and was canonized in 1900, becoming the Patron Saint of impossible causes and reconciliation.
